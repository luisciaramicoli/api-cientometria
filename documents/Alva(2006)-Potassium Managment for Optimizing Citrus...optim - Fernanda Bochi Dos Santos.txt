Titulo: Potassium management for optimizing citrus production and quality

Resumo: Potassium (K) is a highly mobile element in plants and is involved in processes ranging from cellular level to xylem and phloem transport. It is an essential element that plays a vital role in: enzyme activation, protein synthesis, stomatal regulation, internal pH stabilization, photosynthesis, turgor-dependent processes, and translocation of metabolites. Although citrus leaves rarely show visual K deficiency symptoms, fruit yield and quality are significantly influenced by K nutrition. High levels of K result in larger fruits with thick rinds and coarse texture, while K deficiency leads to smaller fruits with thin rinds. For juice quality, higher K levels are associated with increased acidity, and lower K reduces juice acidity. This article reviews the effects of K nutrition on fruit production, postharvest quality, and juice quality in citrus. It also discusses current recommendations for K fertilization based on soil and leaf analyses.

Caracteristicas do solo e região (escrever): Estudos conduzidos em diversas regiões produtoras de citros, incluindo Flórida (EUA), Brasil (especialmente São Paulo, com solos tropicais ácidos, pH 4.8-5.5) e África do Sul. Os tipos de solo variam de arenosos (e.g., Astatula fine sand, Sunniland fine sand) a argilo-arenosos (clay loam), e também solos calcários (pH 7.2-8.0). Drenagem adequada é um fator importante; o K está sujeito a perdas por lixiviação em solos arenosos.
