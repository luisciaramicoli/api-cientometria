Titulo: Fluoride Exposure Compromises Gas Exchange of Plants
Subtítulo: 
Ano: 2013
Número de citações recebidas (Google Scholar): 11
Palavras-chave: Fluoride Pollution; Gas Exchange; Citrus; Coffee; Bioindicator Species
Resumo: Fluorine (Fí) stands out for its phytotoxic potential, because it accumulates in plants, changes enzymes activity, reduces chlorophyll content and, consequently, affects growth and yield of crop plants. An experiment was conducted to evaluate the effects of Fíon leaf gas exchange in coffee and sweet orange plants, compared to sensitive (gladiolus) and tolerant (ryegrass) reference species. Plants grown in pots were exposed to Fí in a semi-open mist chamber. The experimental design was completely randomized with treatments defined by the combination of plant species and two intensities of exposure to atmospheric F, with nebulization of HF solutions (low = 0.065 mmol·mí3 and high = 0.260 mmol·mí3) in a mist chamber, as well as with non-exposed control samples. CO2 assimilation (A), transpiration (E), stomatal conductance (gs) and chlorophyll fluorescence rates were measured after 27 days of treatment application. The leaf gas exchange variables in ryegrass and orange plants did not vary in response to the increase in atmospheric F, while an increase in gs and E values was observed in gladiolus and coffee plants. A decrease in A and potential quantum efficiency of photosystem II (Fv/Fm) was found for gladiolus plants. On the contrary, an increase of A for coffee plants was associated with the apparent effect previously reported about the loss of leaf stomatal regulation related to the short assessment period of plants in this experiment. Damages caused to the photosynthetic system were reflected in the susceptibility of the evaluated species to the contamination by the element.

Caracteristicas do solo e região (escrever): Experimento conduzido em estufa (greenhouse) no Brasil (Cordeirópolis/Campinas, SP). As plantas foram cultivadas em vasos e expostas a poluição atmosférica por Fluoreto (F¯) em câmara de névoa semiaberta. Temperaturas médias variaram de 17°C a 30°C. O estudo não detalha características específicas do solo de cultivo, apenas o ambiente experimental.
ferramentas e técnicas (seleção): Câmara de névoa semiaberta (semi-open mist chamber); Analisador de gás infravermelho (IRGA - LI-6400) para trocas gasosas; Fluorômetro modulado (PAM 2000) para fluorescência de clorofila (Fv/Fm); Análise de Variância (ANOVA) e Teste de Duncan (SAS).
nutrientes (seleção): Flúor (F)
estratégias de fornecimento de nutrientes (seleção): Aplicação atmosférica/foliar (nebulização de solução de HF em câmara de névoa) em concentrações baixas (0.065 mmol·m⁻³) e altas (0.260 mmol·m⁻³).
grupos de culturas (seleção): Frutas (Citrino); Bebidas; Forragem; Ornamentais
culturas presentes (seleção): Café (Coffea arabica L. cv. Obatã IAC 1669-20); Laranjeira Doce (Citrus sinensis (L.) Osbeck cv. Pera); Azevém (Lolium multiflorum Lam.); Gladíolo (Gladiolus spp. var. White Goddess)
FEEDBACK DO CURADOR (escrever): Artigo científico de alta relevância que detalha os efeitos fitotóxicos do Flúor (poluição atmosférica) na fisiologia de plantas agrícolas perenes (café e laranja) e espécies bioindicadoras (azevém e gladíolo), avaliando trocas gasosas e fluorescência de clorofila. O estudo é fundamental para a compreensão da sensibilidade de diferentes culturas à poluição ambiental.
