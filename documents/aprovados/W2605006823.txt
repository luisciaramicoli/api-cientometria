Titulo: Influence of foliar application of polyamines on growth, gas-exchange characteristics, and chlorophyll fluorescence in Bakraii citrus under saline conditions
Subtítulo: 
Ano: 2017
Número de citações recebidas (Google Scholar): 63
Palavras-chave: Spermidine, Putrescine, Chlorophyll fluorescence, Spermine, Salinity
Resumo: The effects of polyamines (PAs) on salt stress in Bakraii (Citrus reticulata Ă Citrus limetta) seedlings were studied. Foliar treatments by putrescine (Put), spermidine (Spd), and spermine (Spm) (0, 0.5, and 1 mM) were applied during the salinity period (0 and 75 mM of NaCl). PA-treated seedlings showed a lower content of Na+ and Cl- in leaves. Application of PAs increased net photosynthetic rate in salt-stressed plants and it contributed to the enhanced growth parameters. PAs application considerably induced growth improvement in Bakraii seedlings which was found to be associated with reduced electrolyte leakage, increased relative water content, chlorophyll fluorescence parameters, activities of key antioxidant enzymes, as well as increased photosynthetic pigment concentration under saline regime. These results showed the promising use of PAs, especially of Spd and Spm, for reducing the negative effects of salinity stress and improving the growth of citrus seedlings.

Caracteristicas do solo e região (escrever): O estudo foi realizado sob condições de casa de vegetação (greenhouse) em Urmia e Isfahan, Irã. A região é tipicamente árida e semiárida, propensa à salinidade. O solo utilizado para o plantio era argilo-lodoso (loamy clay) com pH 7.1 e Condutividade Elétrica (CE) de 0.54 dS m⁻¹.

FEEDBACK DO CURADOR (escrever): Documento altamente relevante para a agronomia, focado na mitigação do estresse salino em mudas de citros (Bakraii, Citrus reticulata × Citrus limetta) através da aplicação foliar de poliaminas (PAs), com destaque para a espermidina e espermina. Os resultados demonstram que as PAs melhoram significativamente o crescimento, a fotossíntese (PN, gs) e o balanço iônico, reduzindo a toxicidade de Na+ e Cl- nas folhas.
