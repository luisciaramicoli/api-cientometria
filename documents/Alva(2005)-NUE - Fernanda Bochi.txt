Resumo: Nitrogen (N) is the most important nutrient for plant growth and production. Nitrogen uptake efficiency is dependent on a number of factors. Water management influences the transformation of N sources applied to the soil and transport of the nitrate form of N in the soil. Nitrate-N is the final product of N transformations and is quite mobile in soils with the water front. Leaching of nitrate below the rootzone is an economic loss and contributes to non-point source pollution of groundwater. In this chapter we summarize the factors influencing the N uptake efficiencies for various crops and production systems, and chemical and biological processes that influence the N transformation or losses. Recent advances leading to development of N and irrigation best management practices that support sustainable crop production and net returns while minimizing the non-point source nitrate pollution of ground water are also discussed.

Caracteristicas do solo e região (escrever): A ênfase principal do estudo é em solos arenosos (sandy soils), como Tavares fine sand e Wabasso sand (Entisols e Spodosols), típicos das principais regiões de produção de citros na Flórida, EUA. Esses solos são notáveis pelo alto teor de areia (>96%), baixa capacidade de retenção de água, baixa capacidade de troca catiônica (CTC) e baixo teor de matéria orgânica. O documento também menciona estudos em solos alcalinos, solos argilosos de drenagem ruim e áreas de cultivo como Pacific Northwest, Nebraska e Texas Central.
